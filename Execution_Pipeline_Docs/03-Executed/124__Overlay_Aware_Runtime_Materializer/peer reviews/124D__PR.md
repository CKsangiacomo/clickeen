## Round-1 gate: BLOCKER + findings (folded into Rev 2)

Round 1: Staff-engineer GREEN, Senior-PM GREEN, **Principal-TPM RED.**

**BLOCKER — circular locale-coordinate dependency (TPM; team must close before
124D executes Slice 4).** 124D Slice 4 step 1 says "use the locale artifact
coordinate selected by 124A," and 124D's precondition gate asserts "124A names
the locale storage coordinate." But the 124A addendum §4.4/§13 explicitly
**deferred** the locale-specific artifact path/URL to **124E**, and 124E's
precondition gate reciprocally requires 124A to have named it. Deadlock: 124D
stores locale packages *before* 124E serves them, yet the storage coordinate was
punted to 124E. Current code confirms the gap — `tokyo-worker/.../package-files.ts`
writes a single fixed coordinate (`accounts/{acct}/instances/{inst}/{file}`) with
**no locale segment**, and `writeInstancePublicPackage` has no locale parameter.
**Resolution (team, PRD edits):** lock the locale artifact storage coordinate
before 124D runs — either a 124A addendum update that actually names it (e.g.
`accounts/{accountPublicId}/instances/{instanceId}/locales/{locale}/...`), or
assign coordinate ownership to 124D's Slice 1 contract-check output with 124A
sign-off. MAMA (the cross-sub-PRD arbiter) should resolve who owns it. (This is
exactly the kind of ownership gap the MAMA review flagged.)

**Non-blocking findings (fold in):**
- **Senior-PM — operator visibility of partial failure.** Slice 5 produces
  completed/skipped/failed coordinates, but no slice claims the operator/admin
  actually *sees* them — Bob's `TranslationsPanel.tsx` currently surfaces only a
  coarse "Translations could not be generated." The honest per-locale evidence
  risks being invisible to the human who triggered a 10-locale generation. Assign
  the operator-facing surface (links to the MAMA admin-locale-status-UX owner).
- **Staff-engineer — restate byte-stability in 124D's Slice 10 closeout.** 124D
  inherits 124C's unclosed condition: `materializeConfigMedia` lives in
  `@clickeen/ck-contracts`, outside `materializerContractVersion`, so locale
  artifact bytes have an uncontrolled serialization input. Restate it as a 124D
  closeout gate, not just the PR.

**Verdict reassessment:** architecturally sound (both other roles GREEN; V6
partial-failure discipline strong; no-public-serving fence correct). **RED only
on the unresolved locale-coordinate contract** — a team edit to 124A/124D that
names the storage coordinate unblocks 124D. PR is GREEN once that blocker is
captured (done here) and the team closes it.
